Research

My research focuses on the development and application of scientific machine learning methods for the modeling, analysis, and interpretation of engineering and physical systems. I am particularly interested in combining physics-based understanding with data-driven approaches to improve predictive reliability and interpretability.

My work spans computational modeling, energy systems analysis, and experimental system design, with an emphasis on using data-informed methods to support engineering decision-making.

Scientific Machine Learning for Physical Systems

A central theme of my research is the use of scientific machine learning techniques to model complex physical and engineering systems. This includes integrating physical constraints, conservation laws, and domain knowledge into machine learning frameworks to enhance model generalization and interpretability.

I focus on simulation-driven datasets, feature engineering, and the development of scalable computational workflows that bridge classical modeling approaches with modern learning architectures.

Energy Systems Modeling and Techno-Economic Analysis

I have conducted research on the modeling and techno-economic evaluation of energy systems, with particular attention to rural and community-scale applications. This work involves system sizing, cost modeling, and performance assessment of distributed energy assets.

The goal of this research is to support informed design decisions that balance technical performance, economic feasibility, and long-term sustainability.

Thermal and Thermochemical Systems

My research experience also includes the design and analysis of thermal and thermochemical systems, such as biomass-based energy conversion technologies and heating systems. This work combines analytical modeling with experimental design and performance evaluation.

Examples include pyrolytic system design, advanced biomass cookstoves, and feasibility analysis of ground-source heat pump systems.

Integration of Data, Models, and Experiments

Across all projects, I aim to integrate data-driven analysis with physics-based models and experimental insights. My research philosophy emphasizes reproducibility, interpretability, and the translation of computational results into practical engineering understanding.

Detailed descriptions of specific projects and outcomes can be found on the Projects and Publications pages.
